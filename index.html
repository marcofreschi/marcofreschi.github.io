Bio

Marco Freschi is a postdoctoral fellow at the Morphing Matter Lab, Department of Mechanical Engineering, UC Berkeley, working under the direction of Professor Lining Yao. His research focuses on engineered bioinspired self-burying seeds: passive actuators that respond to environmental stimuli without electronics.

Before his postdoc, Marco worked as a consultant at Accenture. He then arrived at UC Berkeley thanks to a postdoctoral fellowship sponsored by Enel Foundation and managed by Professor Alessandra Lanzara, after completing his PhD at Politecnico di Milano under the supervision of Professor Giovanni Dotelli, where he studied the tribological and mechanical behavior of metal matrix composites.

His projects include a seeding system for ecological restoration based on wooden structures bioinspired by the Erodium seed mechanism — funded by NSF GCR and covered by CNN and CNN Tech for Good — and a seedpod analysis project evaluating design effects on soil interaction and biodegradability.

Marco has served as PhD Students Representative at Politecnico di Milano and currently as Social Co-chair of the Berkeley Postdoctoral Association.

Marco is actively pursuing faculty and research positions for the 2026–2027 academic year.

Berkeley Postdoctoral Association — As Social Co-chair, Marco organizes events for the postdoc community and leads the Queer Coffee Hour: a monthly meetup dedicated to promoting community and culture within the postdoc and visiting scholar community.

Research Interests

My research sits at the intersection of materials science, engineering design, and sustainability. I study how materials behave and fail, engineer systems inspired by nature, and evaluate the full environmental and social life cycle of what I build — with the goal of creating solutions that perform rigorously and last responsibly.

Materials Science

Rigorous & Functional

Data-driven investigation of functional materials across a broad application spectrum — from tribological metal matrix composites to biodegradable morphing systems.

Design Engineering

Applied & Bioinspired

Application-driven research anchored to real requirements. Nature provides the blueprint — millions of years of optimization translated into engineered solutions at scale.

Sustainability

Economic · Environmental · Social

Sustainability as a design constraint from day one — life-cycle thinking integrated across every project from raw material extraction to end-of-life.
