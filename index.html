Analysis of ball milling time to produce self-lubricating copper-tungsten disulfide composite: best trade-off between tribological performance and electrical properties

M Freschi, M Di Virgilio, L Andena, M Mariani, O Haiko, J Kömi, N Lecis et al.

Next Materials 2, 100083

+ Abstract
Copper–tungsten disulfide (Cu–WS₂) self-lubricating composites were produced via ball milling and powder metallurgy. This study systematically investigates the effect of milling time on microstructure, hardness, electrical conductivity, and tribological performance, identifying the optimal processing window for aerospace sliding contact applications.

Tuning the Parameters of Cu–WS₂ Composite Production via Powder Metallurgy: Evaluation of the Effects on Tribological Properties

M Freschi, L Dragoni, M Mariani, O Haiko, J Kömi, N Lecis, G Dotelli

Lubricants 11 (2), 66

+ Abstract
This work investigates the effects of powder metallurgy processing parameters — compaction pressure, sintering temperature, and WS₂ content — on the tribological and mechanical properties of Cu–WS₂ composites, providing a systematic roadmap for material optimization.

M Freschi, A Paniz, E Cerqueni, G Colella, G Dotelli

📄 Tribology Int. · 2022

2022

Investigation of second phase concentration effects on tribological and electrical properties of Cu–WS₂ composites

M Freschi, M Di Virgilio, O Haiko, M Mariani, L Andena, N Lecis, J Kömi et al.

Tribology International 166, 107357

+ Abstract
The effect of WS₂ concentration on the tribological and electrical performance of copper matrix composites is systematically studied. Results reveal a trade-off between lubrication efficiency and electrical conductivity, with an optimal concentration window identified for sliding contact applications.
📄 Lubricants · 2022

2022

Physico-Mechanical Properties of Metal Matrix Self-Lubricating Composites Reinforced with Traditional and Nanometric Particles

M Freschi, A Arrigoni, O Haiko, L Andena, J Kömi, C Castiglioni, G Dotelli

Lubricants 10 (3), 35

+ Abstract
Comparison of metal matrix composites reinforced with traditional and nanometric WS₂ particles. The study evaluates hardness, friction coefficient, and wear rate, showing that nanometric reinforcements improve tribological performance but require careful process control to prevent agglomeration.

📄 Lubricants · 2021

2021

Employment of Micro- and Nano-WS₂ Structures to Enhance the Tribological Properties of Copper Matrix Composites

M Freschi, M Di Virgilio, G Zanardi, M Mariani, N Lecis, G Dotelli

Lubricants 9 (5), 53

+ Abstract
Micro- and nano-structured WS₂ particles are incorporated into copper matrices to improve tribological performance. The study compares the effect of particle size and morphology on friction, wear, and mechanical properties, demonstrating that nano-WS₂ provides superior lubrication at lower concentrations.

C Moletti, M Freschi, L Primavesi, G Dotelli

3rd PCNS, Milan, Italy

+ Abstract

📄 Exp. Thermal · 2018

2018

Innovative use of fluorescein for the air path study within light-weight wall assemblies

N Hurel, M Freschi, M Pailha, M Woloszyn

Experimental Thermal and Fluid Science 92, 69–88

+ Abstract
A novel fluorescein tracer method is developed to visualize and quantify air infiltration paths in lightweight timber frame wall assemblies. The technique enables non-destructive identification of thermal bridges and leakage points with higher spatial resolution than conventional methods.

M Freschi

Master Thesis, Italy
