About Marco Freschi

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Morphing Materials Lab, Department of Mechanical Engineering, UC Berkeley, working under Prof. Lining Yao. My research focuses on bioinspired morphing materials and stimuli-responsive 4D structures — systems that adapt, transform, and respond to their environment without electronics.

Before Berkeley, I completed my PhD at Politecnico di Milano, where I studied the tribological and mechanical behavior of metal matrix composites, with emphasis on powder metallurgy and solid-state processing. This work established the materials science foundation I now apply to soft and active matter.

My current projects include bioinspired seeding systems for ecological restoration — soft structures that mimic plant seed dispersal mechanisms — and hygroscopic 4D composites that change shape in response to moisture and temperature. This work has been covered by CNN and funded through a PPFP Fellowship and the NSF TTP-T grant.

I am also developing MEridian, a proposed interdisciplinary postdoc collaborative initiative within UC Berkeley ME, aimed at fostering cross-lab research, industry connections, and postdoc community.
