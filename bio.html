Bio

About Marco Freschi

Marco Freschi is a postdoctoral fellow at the Morphing Matter Lab, Department of Mechanical Engineering, UC Berkeley, working under the direction of Professor Lining Yao. His research focuses on engineered bioinspired self-burying seeds: passive actuators that respond to environmental stimuli without electronics.

Marco arrived at UC Berkeley thanks to a postdoctoral fellowship sponsored by Enel Foundation and managed by Professor Alessandra Lanzara, after completing his PhD at Politecnico di Milano, where he studied the tribological and mechanical behavior of metal matrix composites, with emphasis on powder metallurgy processing and material design. This work established his rigorous materials engineering foundation that he now applies to his current research.

His projects include a seeding system for ecological restoration based on wooden structures with an architecture bioinspired by the Erodium seed mechanism — funded by NSF GCR and covered by CNN and CNN Tech for Good — and a seedpod analysis project evaluating design effects on soil interaction and biodegradability.

Marco is also active in campus life as Social Co-chair of the Berkeley Postdoctoral Association, and is developing MEridian, a proposed interdisciplinary postdoc initiative within UC Berkeley ME.
