Research Statement

Three Pillars · Three Intersections
Materials Science

Rigorous & Functional

Data-driven investigation of materials across a broad application spectrum. From metal matrix composites to biodegradable seedpod structures, the approach remains the same: characterize, model, and engineer material behavior with precision.

Design Engineering

Applied & Bioinspired

Application-driven research anchored to real requirements. Nature provides the blueprint — millions of years of optimization distilled into mechanisms that engineering can translate, refine, and deploy at scale.

Sustainability

Economic · Environmental · Social

Sustainability integrated as a design constraint, not an afterthought. Every system is evaluated across its full life cycle — from raw material sourcing to end-of-life soil integration — with economic and social impact in scope.

Materials × Design

Materials Design

Translating functional requirements into material architectures — selecting, processing, and structuring matter to achieve targeted mechanical, biological, or environmental responses.

Design × Sustainability

Life Cycle Design

Embedding life cycle thinking into the design process from day one — ensuring that form, function, and environmental performance are co-optimized rather than traded off.

Materials × Sustainability

Life Cycle Assessment

Quantitative evaluation of environmental impact from cradle to grave. Applied to biodegradable seeding systems to guide material selection and validate sustainability claims with data.

Research Projects

Active & Completed
01

Bioinspired Seeding Systems

Nature has spent millions of years perfecting seed dispersal. This project develops morphing structures — inspired by dandelion, maple samara, and Erodium seeds — that self-deploy, orient, and anchor upon contact with soil. The goal: scalable, biodegradable systems for ecological restoration and precision agriculture.

02

Life-cycle Evaluation of Seedpod Biodegradable Materials

From raw material extraction to end-of-life soil integration — this project applies life-cycle assessment frameworks to biodegradable seedpod-inspired structures. The goal is to quantify environmental trade-offs and guide material selection for ecological restoration systems.

03

Metal Matrix Composites & Tribology

PhD research at Politecnico di Milano investigating the tribological and mechanical behavior of advanced MMCs. Work spanned powder metallurgy, solid-state processing, and multiscale characterization of wear mechanisms — establishing a rigorous materials science foundation for the subsequent pivot to morphing systems.
