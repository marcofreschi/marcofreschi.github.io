Research Statement

Research Statement

Engineering materials that perform, endure, and sustain

My research connects materials science, engineering design, and sustainability into a single coherent practice. I investigate how materials behave under mechanical and environmental stress, translate biological strategies into functional engineered systems, and evaluate the full life-cycle impact of what I build — from raw material to end-of-life soil integration.

Materials Science

Rigorous, data-driven investigation of functional materials. Material characterization techniques are used to understand the properties that govern the mechanisms and performance of a material, in order to exploit them most effectively across different applications.

Design Engineering

Application-driven research anchored to real requirements. Nature has already solved many of the world's most complex challenges — drawing inspiration from these solutions and translating them into scalable, engineered systems is the core of bioinspired design.

Sustainability

Environmental, economic, and social dimensions integrated into the design stage. Life-cycle approach applied to systems, quantifying trade-offs, validating impact, and guiding material selection.

Research Projects

01

Bioinspired Seeding Systems

Active
Nature has spent millions of years perfecting seed dispersal. This project develops morphing structures — inspired by dandelion, maple samara, and Erodium seeds — that self-deploy, orient, and anchor upon contact with soil. The goal: scalable, biodegradable systems for ecological restoration and precision agriculture.
