Research Statement

Engineering materials that
perform, endure, and sustain

My research connects materials science, engineering design, and sustainability into a single coherent practice. I investigate how materials behave under mechanical and environmental stress, translate biological strategies into functional engineered systems, and evaluate the full life-cycle impact of what I build — from raw material to end-of-life soil integration.

Materials Science

Rigorous, data-driven investigation of functional materials — from tribological metal matrix composites to biodegradable morphing systems. Powder metallurgy, multiscale characterization, wear mechanics.

Design Engineering

Application-driven research anchored to real requirements. Nature provides the blueprint — seed dispersal mechanisms, soil penetration, hygroscopic actuation — translated into scalable engineered solutions.

Sustainability

Environmental, economic, and social dimensions integrated from day one. Life-cycle assessment applied to biodegradable seeding systems — quantifying trade-offs, validating impact, guiding material selection.

Research Projects

01

Bioinspired Seeding Systems

Active

Nature has spent millions of years perfecting seed dispersal. This project develops morphing structures — inspired by dandelion, maple samara, and Erodium seeds — that self-deploy, orient, and anchor upon contact with soil. The goal: scalable, biodegradable systems for ecological restoration and precision agriculture.

Funding

NSF GCR · Bulat Utemuratov Foundation

Lab

Morphing Matter Lab, UC Berkeley

Techniques

Collaborations

Cornell University · Univ. of Pennsylvania · Syracuse University · James Madison University

02

Pod Penetration in Soil: Analysis of Drilling Efficiency

Active

Efficient penetration of biodegradable carriers into soil is critical for restoration and reforestation applications. This project evaluates how system parameters — approach speed, rotation, and pod geometry — affect drilling efficiency and successful soil anchoring.

Funding

NSF GCR · Bulat Utemuratov Foundation

Lab

Morphing Matter Lab, UC Berkeley

Techniques

3D printing · Field testing · Soil mechanics · Materials degradation

Collaborations

Univ. of Pennsylvania · UC Berkeley · Syracuse University

03

Metal Matrix Composites & Tribology

Completed

PhD research at Politecnico di Milano investigating the tribological and mechanical behavior of advanced MMCs. Work spanned powder metallurgy, solid-state processing, and multiscale characterization of wear mechanisms — establishing a rigorous materials science foundation for the subsequent pivot to morphing systems.

Funding
